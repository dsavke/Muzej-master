Department of Ethnology

The Department of Ethnology is one of the most compelling and richest departments of the RS Museum (Department of Ethnology and Ethnography), which is understandable considering its establishment and activities from the very beginning. Artefacts of these collections in the permanent exhibition show the material culture of the former Bosanska Krajina. The first unit consists of environmental demonstrations of traditional crafts. There are presented tools and products of pottery, blacksmith, silversmith, coppersmith, leather, vats and carpenter trade. In the second unit (traditional architecture and household furniture) it is shown a cross-section of one-piece chalet and a city room of Oriental type. The interior of the exhibit is filled with the appropriate useful objects. The third unit displays artefacts for traditional way of earning: agriculture, cattle breeding, fruit growing, apiculture, hunting and fishing. The exhibited costumes in the Collections of Textiles, as well as other handicraft items are a part of the rich folk art and handicraft of the northwestern Bosnia people whereof traditional costumes of Bosanska Krajina, both rural and urban, attract with beauty. The exhibited rural folk costumes are Orthodox folk costumes from Imljani, Orthodox folk costumes from Janj and the Catholic folk costume from Debeljaci. The presented city costumes are city costumes of the Christian population from Banja Luka and Mrkonjic Grad. The exhibited textiles handicrafts are: braids, handkerchiefs, crochet tablecloths and particular parts of folk costumes.
